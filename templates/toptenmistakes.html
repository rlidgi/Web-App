Top 10 Resume Mistakes to Avoid (and How to Fix Them)

Your resume is your first impression. But even great candidates make small mistakes that get their applications rejected. This guide walks you through the 10 most common resume errors—and exactly how to fix them.

1. Spelling and Grammar Errors

Why it matters: Typos suggest carelessness.

Fix it: Use spellcheck, then proofread manually. Try tools like Grammarly or ask a friend to review it.

2. Using One Generic Resume for All Jobs

Why it matters: Recruiters spot a one-size-fits-all resume instantly.

Fix it: Tailor each resume by matching skills and experience to the job posting.

3. Including Irrelevant Work Experience

Why it matters: It distracts from your qualifications.

Fix it: Only list experience that is relevant or demonstrates transferable skills.

4. Not Quantifying Achievements

Why it matters: Metrics show real results.

Fix it: Use numbers: “Led a team of 5 and increased delivery speed by 30%.”

5. Poor Formatting

Why it matters: A cluttered resume is hard to scan.

Fix it: Use clean fonts, consistent spacing, and bold headers. Avoid visuals or tables.

6. Overusing Buzzwords

Why it matters: Phrases like “go-getter” or “synergy” lack substance.

Fix it: Replace with real actions and accomplishments.

7. Making It Too Long (or Too Short)

Why it matters: Recruiters spend just seconds scanning.

Fix it: 1 page for early career, 2 max for experienced pros.

8. Ignoring ATS (Applicant Tracking Systems)

Why it matters: Your resume may never reach a human.

Fix it: Use standard headers, include keywords, and avoid graphics or tables.

9. Leaving Out a Summary or Objective

Why it matters: A summary gives recruiters quick context.

Fix it: Write a short summary showing your value and intent.

10. Including Personal Information

Why it matters: Unnecessary details can lead to bias.

Fix it: Stick to name, email, phone, LinkedIn, and optionally a portfolio or GitHub.

Final Thought

A resume isn’t just a list of jobs—it’s a marketing tool. Avoid these 10 mistakes, and you’re far more likely to earn that callback.

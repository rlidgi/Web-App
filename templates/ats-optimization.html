What Is an ATS?

An ATS is software used by employers to scan resumes for keywords and rank candidates based on relevance. If your resume doesn’t include the right terms or uses a poor format, it might be rejected automatically—before a human sees it.

Common ATS Traps

  • ❌ Fancy designs with images, columns, or charts
  • ❌ Uncommon fonts or symbols
  • ❌ Headers like "Professional Story" instead of "Experience"
  • ❌ Saving your resume as an image or incompatible file type

How to Beat the ATS

  • ✔️ Use standard headers like "Experience", "Education", "Skills"
  • ✔️ Stick to .docx or clean .pdf formats
  • ✔️ Match the job post’s wording (especially job titles and key skills)
  • ✔️ Avoid tables, images, or graphics—ATS bots can’t read them

ATS-Friendly Resume Checklist

  • ✅ Simple, clean layout
  • ✅ Job-specific keywords included
  • ✅ Accurate job titles and formatting
  • ✅ No photos, icons, or graphics
  • ✅ Saved as .docx or .pdf

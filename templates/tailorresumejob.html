How to Tailor Your Resume to a Specific Job Description

Recruiters can tell instantly when you haven’t customized your resume—and that’s often enough to land your application in the “no” pile. This guide shows how to tailor your resume so it gets noticed by both humans and ATS.

1. Start With a Solid Master Resume

Create a “master resume” that includes all your experience, skills, and education. From there, you can copy the most relevant pieces into each tailored version.

2. Analyze the Job Description Carefully

Highlight these elements as you read:

  • Required qualifications
  • Preferred skills
  • Industry-specific terms
  • Responsibilities and duties
  • Tools or technologies

These help guide the keywords you include in your resume.

3. Match Your Summary to the Role

Customize your professional summary to reflect the job title and highlight top qualifications.

Generic:
“Experienced marketing professional skilled in social media and analytics.”

Tailored:
“Data-driven Digital Marketing Specialist with 5+ years of experience in SEO, Google Ads, and performance analytics, seeking to drive measurable growth for XYZ Company.”

4. Customize Your Skills Section

If the job lists “project management, Salesforce, and collaboration”—make sure those appear in your skills list if you have them.

5. Align Your Experience With Their Priorities

Use their phrasing where appropriate. For example:

Job ad: “Experience improving operational efficiency”

Your bullet: “Streamlined workflows and reduced processing time by 25% through automation tools.”

6. Include Relevant Keywords for ATS

ATS software filters resumes by keyword. Include exact matches for:

  • Job titles
  • Certifications
  • Software/tools
  • Key responsibilities

7. Adjust Job Titles If Needed (Carefully)

It’s okay to adjust a title slightly to better match what you actually did.

Example: Marketing Associate (Marketing Coordinator Duties)

8. Cut What’s Not Relevant

If a job isn’t relevant to the role you want, trim it down or remove it—especially if you're over one page.

9. Use a Cover Letter to Strengthen the Match

It’s another opportunity to explain how your background fits the job and organization. Use it to connect your value to their needs.

10. Final Check: Read It From the Recruiter’s Perspective

  • Does this resume clearly show I can do this specific job?
  • Are the most relevant skills and achievements easy to find?
  • Am I speaking their language?
Ready to stand out?

Tailoring your resume takes more effort, but it drastically increases your chances of landing interviews.
